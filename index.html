SGA Testimony

Why this campaign matters to me

When I joined SGA as a freshman, I was nervous and quiet, but I still loved being involved. From helping paint the Class of 2027 wall to attending meetings and events, SGA quickly became a huge part of my high school experience.

Over the past three years, I’ve grown alongside SGA through orientations, meetings, and countless after-school canned food drive sessions. SGA helped me find my voice and passion for leadership, and now I want to use that experience to give even more students ways to belong, contribute, and lead.

When I transferred from Centennial HS to Mt. Hebron HS, the first thing I did was head straight to the SGA table. Since then, I’ve worked not only at the school level, but also with county and state student leadership opportunities, and I’d be honored to keep making change as president.

Bringing island energy to SGA

My campaign pillars

Volunteer Island

Service and volunteer opportunities

I want every student to have a chance to get involved, even in small ways, through more accessible service opportunities and stronger participation in events like the canned food drive.

Spirit Island

Engaging events and an active SGA

I don’t want SGA energy to fade after the start of the school year. I want events and momentum to continue through winter and spring too.

Voice Island

Student ideas that shape action

This campaign is about listening as much as leading, so I want regular feedback loops, surveys, and idea collection to become part of how SGA works.

Leadership Island

Prepared, passionate representation

The SGA president represents the student body at meetings, public events, district occasions, and county councils, and I’m ready for that responsibility.

Questions students might ask

What does the SGA president actually do?

The president prepares for and presides over SGA meetings, represents the student body, works with administration, coordinates the canned food drive, and attends county student council meetings.

What makes this campaign different?

This campaign focuses on staying active all year and expanding opportunities for student involvement.
